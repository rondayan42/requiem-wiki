General Information

Monsters that are 15 or more levels lower than you will not attack unless provoked (confirmated).

Other than that, monsters might have several kinds of aggro: groups might be linked, meaning that if you attack one all will wake up (you can often detect which monsters are linked in this way when their idle animation is synchronized.) Other monsters might get aggro when they see you attacking something (often used for "mothers" attacking you when you attack a "young"), or they might "call their comrades" for help. There is still a third kind although I have seen it only once or twice. When attacking "mother" monsters like mother basilisk, the young basilisk may join in to attack you if there are two or more of them in close proximity. I have not had the chance to really notice the exact number, but I know for sure it is more than one. It has happened with two young basilisk in close proximity. It may have been a one off chance, but it has happened to me twice so far once with two young basilisk and three young basilisk, and I've seen this happen to other players as well.

More confirmation on the third type of aggro, today I attacked a regular sized Gropie with two little ones next to it. All three started attacking me while another large Gropie nearby didn't even look twice. If you'd like to check this one out for yourself, the Gropie parent and two children are right behind (north of) the Memorial Tower in Crescent, near Valdes Village.
